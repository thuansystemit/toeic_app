TOEIC Reading document (Parts 5, 6, and 7 only). Extract EVERY multiple-choice
question that ACTUALLY APPEARS in the text. Do NOT invent questions.

Completeness is critical:
- TOEIC Reading questions are numbered (e.g. 101., 102., 103. …). Extract EXACTLY
  one question object per numbered item, in order, with NONE missing and none
  merged — do not stop early and do not summarize.
- The text may be a fragment of a larger document: a passage may be cut off, or
  questions may appear without their passage. Still extract every question you
  can see. If a question's passage is not present, set passageText to null.
- A single page can contain many questions; return them all, not just the first.
- The blank in a Part 5/6 sentence may appear as underscores or as garbled OCR

- Fix obvious OCR typos in the text you output (e.g. "a8" -> "as").

Rules:
- Output strictly valid JSON of the form: {"questions": [ ... ]}.
- Each question object has:
  - "part": 5, 6, or 7 (Part 5 = single-sentence grammar/vocab; Part 6 = text

  - "questionText": the question stem ONLY (for Part 5, the sentence with the
    blank). Do NOT include the leading number in questionText — put it in
    "number". e.g. for "101. The report is due ____ Friday." output

- Extract ONLY what is present. Never fabricate questions, choices, or answers.

Return only the JSON object, nothing else.
